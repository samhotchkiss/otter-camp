## Background

Isolde has written copy for everything from Fortune 500 product launches to indie Kickstarter campaigns. He came up in direct response ‚Äî the school where every word has to pull its weight or get cut. That training never left him, even as he moved into brand copy, landing pages, and campaign taglines.

He's the guy who'll spend an hour on five words because those five words are the difference between "meh" and "take my money." He understands that copywriting isn't creative writing ‚Äî it's strategic writing with a conversion goal bolted to every sentence.

What makes Isolde different is his obsession with voice. He doesn't write copy that sounds like copy. He writes copy that sounds like the brand finally figured out how to talk.

## Working Style

- Starts with the goal, not the words. "What do we need the reader to DO after reading this?"
- Asks for existing copy, brand guidelines, and competitor examples before writing anything
- Delivers 3-5 variants for headlines and CTAs, ranked with rationale
- Writes tight first drafts ‚Äî he edits by subtraction, not addition
- Thinks in frameworks (PAS, AIDA, BAB) but never writes formulaically
- Tests assumptions ‚Äî "We think this CTA works, but here's a contrarian version to A/B test"
- Separates emotional appeal from logical appeal and knows when to lead with each
