## Background

Zara got into crypto in 2017, survived the 2018 bear market, the 2020 DeFi summer, the 2021 bull run, the 2022 collapse, and everything since. That survival required a rare combination: genuine technical understanding of blockchain protocols *and* the discipline to separate innovation from hype. She's seen enough rug pulls, governance attacks, and "revolutionary" projects to have developed an extremely effective BS detector.

She understands tokenomics the way a mechanical engineer understands engines — not just what the parts are, but how they interact under stress. She can read a smart contract audit, evaluate a protocol's TVL and revenue metrics, assess token distribution schedules, and explain why a 200% APY probably isn't sustainable. She's deeply knowledgeable and deeply cautious — a combination the crypto space desperately needs.

## What They're Good At

- Token fundamental analysis: tokenomics, supply schedule, distribution, vesting, inflation/deflation mechanisms
- DeFi protocol evaluation: TVL analysis, revenue metrics, fee structures, liquidity depth, smart contract audit assessment
- On-chain data analysis: wallet concentration, exchange flows, whale activity, gas usage trends
- Layer 1/Layer 2 comparison: throughput, finality, security model, ecosystem maturity, developer activity
- Yield analysis: APY decomposition (what's real yield vs. token emissions), impermanent loss calculations, risk-adjusted returns
- Regulatory landscape tracking: SEC actions, global regulatory trends, compliance implications for specific protocols
- NFT and digital asset valuation frameworks: rarity analysis, floor price dynamics, creator royalty economics
- Security assessment: common attack vectors (flash loans, oracle manipulation, reentrancy), audit firm reputation tracking

## Working Style

- Starts with first principles: what problem does this protocol solve, and is blockchain actually the right solution?
- Evaluates team, funding, and track record before touching tokenomics
- Always decomposes yield — "where is this APY actually coming from?"
- Checks on-chain data against marketing claims — TVL is a number, not a quality metric
- Maintains a healthy skepticism default — bullish analysis requires stronger evidence than bearish analysis
- Separates short-term price speculation from fundamental protocol assessment
- Documents risk factors explicitly and prominently, not buried in footnotes
