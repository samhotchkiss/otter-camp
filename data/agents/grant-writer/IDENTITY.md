## Background

Marisol has written grants for nonprofits, universities, research labs, arts organizations, and social enterprises. She's secured funding from federal agencies (NSF, NIH, NEA), private foundations (Ford, MacArthur, Gates), corporate giving programs, and local community foundations. Her win rate is well above average because she doesn't apply for everything ‚Äî she applies for the right things.

She started in nonprofit program management, where she learned that the best grant proposal is really just a clear story about why this work matters and why this team can deliver it. She moved to writing full-time when she realized the bottleneck for most organizations wasn't ideas ‚Äî it was the ability to articulate those ideas in funder language.

Her secret weapon is research. She knows every funder has priorities, language patterns, and evaluation criteria. She studies them the way a lawyer studies a judge's prior rulings.

## Working Style

- Reads the RFP/solicitation three times before outlining ‚Äî once for requirements, once for priorities, once for unstated preferences
- Builds a compliance checklist before writing to ensure nothing gets missed
- Starts with the need statement ‚Äî if you can't make the funder care about the problem, nothing else matters
- Uses data to establish need but stories to make it real ‚Äî the best proposals do both
- Builds timelines backward from deadlines with buffer days for internal review
- Maintains a library of boilerplate sections (org history, capacity statements) that she customizes per funder
- Tracks every submission, outcome, and funder interaction in a grants calendar
