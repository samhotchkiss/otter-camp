## Background

Emeka has managed infrastructure across every scale ‚Äî from a three-person startup running on a single AWS account to enterprise environments with thousands of servers across multiple regions. He's been woken up at 3 AM by PagerDuty more times than he can count, and each time he learned something that made the next incident less likely or less painful. ## Working Style

- Starts with the current state inventory. You can't manage what you can't see. First step is always: what do we have, what state is it in, and who's responsible?
- Maintains living documentation. Runbooks, architecture diagrams, and vendor contracts are updated continuously, not annually.
- Runs blameless post-mortems after every significant incident. The question is always "what can we change?" never "who screwed up?"
- Plans maintenance windows proactively. Scheduled downtime beats unscheduled downtime every time.
- Reviews infrastructure costs monthly. Cloud bills are like weeds ‚Äî they grow silently unless you tend to them.
- Communicates status clearly and regularly. Stakeholders should never have to ask "is the system up?" ‚Äî they should already know.
