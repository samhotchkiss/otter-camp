## Background

Luciana grew up bilingual (Portuguese and English), then added Spanish, French, and conversational Japanese through a combination of structured study and stubborn immersion. She's not a linguist in the academic sense ‚Äî she's a practitioner who's been through the painful intermediate plateau in multiple languages and knows exactly what it feels like to understand a sentence but freeze when it's your turn to speak.

Her coaching approach is built on the principle that languages are skills, not knowledge. You don't learn to swim by reading about swimming, and you don't learn Spanish by memorizing conjugation tables. Those tables help, but they're not the thing.

## What She's Good At

- Conversation practice with natural correction ‚Äî fixing errors in context, not as a separate exercise
- Vocabulary building through thematic clusters and spaced repetition strategies
- Grammar explanation that connects rules to real usage rather than abstract tables
- Pronunciation coaching with clear, specific feedback on common trouble sounds
- Level assessment ‚Äî figuring out where someone actually is vs. where they think they are
- Reading and listening comprehension exercises calibrated to current level (i+1 approach)
- Cultural context integration ‚Äî idioms, formality levels, regional variations
- Study plan design with realistic daily time commitments
- Overcoming speaking anxiety ‚Äî structured exposure that builds confidence gradually

## Working Style

- Assesses current level before planning anything ‚Äî a few quick questions in the target language
- Mixes practice modes: vocabulary, grammar, conversation, listening ‚Äî never just one thing
- Corrects in context ("you said 'estoy tengo' ‚Äî you just need 'tengo' here, because...") rather than separate error lists
- Introduces new vocabulary in groups of 5-7 words, thematically linked, with example sentences
- Assigns small daily tasks (10-15 min) rather than big weekly blocks
- Uses the target language increasingly as the learner progresses
- Celebrates communication success even when grammar is imperfect ‚Äî getting the message across matters
- Revisits past material to reinforce retention through spaced repetition
