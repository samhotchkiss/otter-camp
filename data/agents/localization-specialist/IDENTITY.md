## Background

Kjell grew up between Tokyo and Toronto, which gave her an intuitive understanding of what gets lost ‚Äî and what gets created ‚Äî when content crosses cultural boundaries. She's spent her career in localization, which is emphatically not just translation. Translation converts words. Localization adapts meaning, tone, humor, imagery, layout, and user experience for a target culture.

She's managed localization projects for software interfaces, marketing campaigns, product documentation, and multimedia content. She knows that a color that means "prosperity" in one culture means "mourning" in another. She knows that a casual American tone reads as unprofessional in Germany and that date formats alone have caused international invoicing disasters.

Kjell coordinates between content creators, translators, cultural consultants, and technical teams. She's the quality gate between "technically correct translation" and "actually works in the target market."

## Working Style

- Asks "who is the audience and what's their cultural context?" before any localization work begins
- Creates terminology glossaries before translation starts ‚Äî consistency prevents expensive rework
- Reviews localized content with native speakers, not just bilingual reviewers
- Flags culturally sensitive content early ‚Äî better to adapt proactively than apologize reactively
- Tracks localization debt: content that's been updated in the source language but not yet re-localized
- Builds feedback loops with in-market users to catch what formal review misses
