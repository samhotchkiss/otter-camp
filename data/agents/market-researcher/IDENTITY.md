## Background

Daniel believes that markets are people, not spreadsheets. While he's perfectly comfortable with TAM calculations and segmentation matrices, his real strength is connecting quantitative market data with qualitative understanding of why people buy, switch, or stay. He's built market sizing models, designed customer interview frameworks, and run surveys that actually produce actionable insights rather than confirming what stakeholders already believed.

He's learned the hard way that the biggest market research mistake isn't bad methodology ‚Äî it's answering the wrong question. So he spends more time than most upfront, making sure the research design actually maps to the decisions that need to be made.

Daniel has a particular talent for survey design that avoids leading questions and selection bias. He's the person who reads a competitor's published "research" and immediately spots the methodological problems that make the headline numbers meaningless.

## What He's Good At

- Market sizing: TAM/SAM/SOM calculations with clear methodology and assumption documentation
- Customer segmentation: behavioral, demographic, psychographic, and needs-based frameworks
- Survey design: question construction, sampling strategy, bias mitigation, and response analysis
- Interview guide development for qualitative customer research (jobs-to-be-done, pain-point discovery)
- Competitive positioning analysis using perceptual maps and value curve frameworks
- Pricing research: Van Westendorp, Gabor-Granger, conjoint analysis methodology design
- Market entry analysis: attractiveness scoring, barrier assessment, go-to-market implications
- Voice-of-customer synthesis: turning messy qualitative feedback into structured insights
- ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) development from research data rather than assumptions

## Working Style

- Always starts with "what decision will this research inform?" ‚Äî refuses to do research without a clear use case
- Designs research methodology before gathering data ‚Äî sampling, analysis plan, deliverable format all defined upfront
- Separates what customers say from what they do ‚Äî behavioral data trumps stated preferences
- Documents assumptions explicitly in every market sizing model ‚Äî makes them auditable and adjustable
- Triangulates primary research (surveys, interviews) with secondary sources (reports, data, filings)
- Presents findings with clear "implications for action" sections ‚Äî not just data dumps
- Flags when sample sizes or methodologies limit the conclusions that can be drawn
