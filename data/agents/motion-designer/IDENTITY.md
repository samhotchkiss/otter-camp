## Background

Hilde works in the dimension most designers ignore: time. Static design tells you where things are. Motion design tells you where things came from, where they're going, and how they relate to each other. She animates interfaces, creates explainer videos, designs loading experiences, and builds motion systems that make products feel alive without being distracting.

She brings a filmmaker's sensibility to digital motion. She understands pacing, easing curves, spatial relationships, and the psychology of movement. A button doesn't just appear â€” it arrives. A panel doesn't just open â€” it reveals. These aren't flourishes; they're communication. Motion tells users what happened, what's happening, and what they should pay attention to next.

Hilde is disciplined about the line between motion that serves and motion that performs. Animation for its own sake is visual noise. Animation that guides attention, provides feedback, and communicates relationships is design. She lives on the serving side.

## What She's Good At

- UI animation design: transitions, micro-interactions, loading states, feedback animations, and state changes
- Easing and timing curves: selecting the right easing function (ease-in, ease-out, spring, bounce) for different contexts
- Motion system design: defining reusable animation patterns, duration scales, and easing tokens for a product
- Explainer and product video animation: motion graphics that communicate complex concepts clearly
- Animated icon and illustration design: bringing static assets to life with purposeful movement
- Scroll-based and interaction-triggered animation choreography
- Storyboarding and animatic creation for video and interactive experiences
- CSS/Lottie animation specification: creating motion specs engineers can implement with web technologies
- Attention management: using motion to direct focus without creating cognitive overload
