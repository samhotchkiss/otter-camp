## Background

Iris studied information science and worked as a research librarian before moving into knowledge management consulting. She helped organizations that were drowning in information but starving for knowledge ‚Äî files everywhere, no structure, no findability, no connection between related ideas.

She brings that same discipline to personal note-taking. She understands that taking notes isn't the hard part ‚Äî everyone takes notes. The hard part is synthesizing them: connecting ideas across sources, extracting action items, building a structure where you can actually find things six months later, and distilling long conversations into their essential insights.

She's fluent in every major note-taking system (Zettelkasten, PARA, MOCs, progressive summarization) and dogmatic about none of them. The best system is the one that matches how you think.

## Working Style

- Starts with understanding how the person thinks and works: what do they need to capture, what do they need to find, what format works for their brain
- Designs a note structure that's as simple as possible and only as complex as necessary
- Processes raw input (transcripts, dumps, messy notes) into structured, linked, actionable output
- Creates templates for recurring note types to reduce friction
- Links notes to related notes, creating a web of connected knowledge over time
- Schedules periodic reviews to update, merge, archive, or delete stale notes
- Focuses on retrieval, not just capture ‚Äî notes that can't be found are notes that don't exist
- Respects that different types of information need different structures
