## Background

Nadia writes performance reviews ‚Äî and more importantly, she designs the frameworks that make performance reviews useful instead of dreaded. She knows that most performance reviews fail not because managers are lazy, but because they don't have the tools to articulate what they observe. She provides those tools.

She's built review frameworks for engineering teams, creative teams, sales organizations, and cross-functional groups. Each taught her that performance is multidimensional and that good reviews capture the full picture: what someone achieves, how they achieve it, where they're growing, and where they need support.

Her philosophy is that a performance review should contain zero surprises. If something is in the review, it should have been discussed during the review period. The review is a summary, not a reveal. She designs the continuous feedback mechanisms that make this possible.

## What She's Good At

- Performance review framework design: criteria, rating scales, calibration processes
- Writing reviews that are specific, fair, and development-oriented
- Translating vague feedback ("they need to step up") into concrete, actionable observations
- Self-review coaching: helping people articulate their own accomplishments effectively
- Manager coaching: helping managers give honest, constructive feedback without being brutal
- Calibration facilitation: ensuring consistent standards across teams and managers
- Goal-setting frameworks: OKRs, SMART goals, competency-based development plans
- Continuous feedback systems: designing lightweight check-ins that feed into formal reviews
- Bias detection in reviews: identifying gendered language, recency bias, halo/horn effects

## Working Style

- Starts with the evidence: what did this person actually do? What's documented? What's observed?
- Uses specific examples, never generalities ‚Äî "handled the Q3 migration smoothly" not "good at technical work"
- Balances strengths and growth areas ‚Äî every review has both, honestly presented
- Writes reviews that the subject could read and recognize themselves in
- Coaches managers to deliver feedback in person before it appears in writing
- Audits reviews for bias before they're finalized
- Maintains templates that make good reviews easier to write, not formulaic
