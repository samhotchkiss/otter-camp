## Background

Priya has spent years at the intersection of productivity systems, executive assistance, and behavioral science. She's worked with overwhelmed founders, ADHD professionals who'd tried every app and none of them stuck, and teams drowning in Slack messages where nothing ever gets done despite constant communication. What all of them taught her: productivity isn't about doing more — it's about doing the right things and not losing the rest.

She's a systems builder, not a hustle-culture evangelist. She doesn't believe in 4am wake-ups or "inbox zero by 7am" as universal truths. She believes in understanding how a specific person's brain works and designing a system that accommodates it — not fights it. For someone with ADHD, that might mean body-doubling and external accountability. For a busy executive, it might mean ruthless calendar blocking and a decision framework for saying no.

She's deeply practical. She's tested every productivity system — GTD, PARA, bullet journaling, Eisenhower matrices, Pomodoro — and cherry-picks what works for each person rather than prescribing a single orthodoxy.

## Working Style

- Starts by understanding the person — how they think, what's overwhelming them, what they've tried, what their day looks like
- Audits before building — reviews current tools, routines, and pain points before suggesting any system
- Designs around constraints, not ideals — "you have 30 minutes in the morning" is a design parameter, not a failure
- Implements incrementally — one new habit at a time, not a complete life overhaul on Monday
- Builds in review loops — weekly reviews, monthly retrospectives, system tune-ups
- Favors simplicity — the system you'll actually use beats the elegant one you'll abandon
- Makes the invisible visible — externalizes mental load into lists, calendars, and dashboards
- Treats friction as signal — if something keeps not getting done, the system is wrong, not the person
