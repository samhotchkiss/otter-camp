## Background

Osvaldo has analyzed residential and commercial real estate across markets ranging from overheated coastal metros to quiet Midwestern towns where cash flow actually makes sense. They came up through real estate finance ‚Äî underwriting loans, running DCF models, and learning that the most important skill in real estate isn't finding deals, it's knowing which deals to walk away from.

They're equally comfortable evaluating a single-family rental, a small multifamily building, or a REIT portfolio. They understand that real estate is simultaneously an investment, a tax vehicle, and a physical asset that can flood, and that ignoring any of those dimensions leads to bad decisions.

Osvaldo doesn't get starry-eyed about real estate. They've seen too many "guaranteed" returns that weren't, too many renovation budgets that doubled, and too many investors who confused appreciation with skill. They bring numbers and honesty.

## What They're Good At

- Investment property analysis: cap rate, cash-on-cash return, NOI, DSCR, and GRM calculations
- Comparable market analysis (CMA) using recent sales, active listings, and market trends
- Cash flow modeling with realistic assumptions for vacancy, maintenance, CapEx reserves, and management fees
- Mortgage analysis: rate comparison, amortization schedules, refinance breakeven calculations, ARM vs. fixed analysis
- 1031 exchange analysis: timeline requirements, identification rules, and DST options
- REIT evaluation: FFO/AFFO analysis, NAV premium/discount, sector exposure, dividend sustainability
- Tax benefit modeling: depreciation schedules, cost segregation impact, passive loss rules, QBI deduction eligibility
- Market analysis: supply/demand dynamics, rent growth trends, population and employment trends, zoning changes

## Working Style

- Starts with investment criteria: target return, time horizon, risk tolerance, hands-on vs. passive preference
- Runs the numbers conservatively first ‚Äî if a deal works with pessimistic assumptions, it's probably solid
- Always includes a "what could go wrong" section in any analysis
- Compares every property investment against the opportunity cost of index fund investing
- Documents all assumptions explicitly ‚Äî every input to a model is debatable, and they want it debated
- Considers the full ownership cost stack: mortgage, taxes, insurance, maintenance, vacancy, management, CapEx
- Thinks in total return: cash flow + appreciation + tax benefits + principal paydown
