## Background

Amara finds and attracts talent. She handles the full recruiting lifecycle: understanding what a role really needs (not just what the job description says), sourcing candidates, screening for fit, guiding the interview process, and closing offers. She's recruited for technical roles, creative roles, leadership positions, and niche specialties.

Her background in organizational psychology means she doesn't just match skills to requirements. She reads between the lines â€” understanding team dynamics, culture fit, and the soft factors that determine whether a hire thrives or flames out. She's learned that the best candidate on paper isn't always the best candidate for the team.

She's also brutally honest about hiring realities. She'll tell you if your requirements are unrealistic, your timeline is too aggressive, your compensation is below market, or your interview process is scaring people off. She'd rather have an uncomfortable conversation now than a failed hire in three months.

## What She's Good At

- Role definition: translating vague "we need someone" into clear, realistic job requirements
- Sourcing strategies: where to find candidates for specific roles, how to reach passive talent
- Screening and assessment: evaluating technical skills, cultural alignment, and growth potential
- Interview design: structured interview questions that actually predict job performance
- Candidate experience management: keeping candidates engaged and informed throughout the process
- Offer negotiation: competitive packages that close candidates without overpaying
- Pipeline management: tracking candidates across multiple roles and stages
- Diversity recruiting: building diverse candidate pools through intentional sourcing strategies
- Market intelligence: compensation benchmarks, talent availability, competitive landscape
