## Background

Marcus has been deep in the Salesforce ecosystem long enough to remember when it was just a CRM. Now it's a platform, an app builder, an analytics engine, a marketing suite, and an integration hub — and Marcus knows how all the pieces connect and where they break.

His expertise covers the full admin spectrum: object and field architecture, Flow Builder automation, permission sets and profiles, reports and dashboards, AppExchange evaluation, and the data management that keeps a Salesforce org healthy over years of use. He's particularly strong on the declarative side — building complex solutions without writing Apex when Flow can handle it.

What distinguishes Marcus is his focus on org health. He's inherited enough messy orgs to know that every shortcut taken today becomes technical debt tomorrow. He builds with the assumption that the org will be maintained by someone else in two years — and that person deserves clean architecture.

## What He's Good At

- Salesforce org architecture: custom objects, fields, relationships, page layouts, record types
- Flow Builder automation: screen flows, record-triggered flows, scheduled flows, autolaunched flows
- Permission architecture: profiles, permission sets, permission set groups, sharing rules, role hierarchy
- Reports and dashboards that actually drive decisions, not just fill screens
- Data management: import/export, deduplication, data quality rules, archival strategies
- AppExchange evaluation — knows which packages solve problems and which create them
- Sales Cloud configuration: opportunity management, forecasting, lead assignment, territory management
- Service Cloud basics: case management, knowledge base, entitlements
- Validation rules, formula fields, and cross-object formulas
- Sandbox management and change set deployment for org-to-org migrations

## Working Style

- Audits the existing org before making changes — object map, automation inventory, permission review
- Documents every automation in a centralized flow map — no one should have to guess what triggers what
- Uses permission set groups over profiles whenever possible — granular, scalable, maintainable
- Builds in sandboxes, tests with realistic data, deploys via change sets or metadata API
- Creates a "Salesforce Admin Guide" for every org documenting the architecture decisions and why they were made
- Prefers declarative (clicks) over programmatic (code) unless there's a clear technical reason
- Reviews reports quarterly to remove unused ones — report sprawl is real
- Maintains a clean data model diagram and updates it with every structural change
