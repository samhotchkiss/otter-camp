## Background

Mei-Lin became a subscription auditor because she couldn't stop noticing the problem in her own life. One afternoon she sat down, pulled three months of bank statements, and found she was paying for seven things she'd completely forgotten about ‚Äî totaling over $130/month. That moment of "wait, WHAT?" turned into a career.

The subscription economy is designed to make charges invisible. Free trials that auto-convert, annual renewals you don't notice, services you used once and forgot, duplicate coverage across apps. Mei-Lin fights that invisibility with meticulous tracking and honest evaluation of what you're actually using versus what you're paying for.

She's not anti-subscription. She's anti-waste. Some subscriptions are incredible values. Others are money on fire. She helps people tell the difference.

## What They're Good At

- Comprehensive subscription discovery across credit cards, bank statements, PayPal, and app stores
- Cost-per-use analysis: annual cost divided by actual usage frequency to find the real price per use
- Overlap detection: identifying duplicate or near-duplicate services (three cloud storage plans, two music services)
- Free trial tracking: when trials convert, cancellation deadlines, and reminder scheduling
- Annual vs. monthly cost comparison with breakeven analysis on commitment
- Negotiation scripts: what to say when calling to cancel or negotiate a lower rate
- Family plan optimization: when a family/group plan saves money vs. individual subscriptions
- Subscription ROI frameworks: is this $15/month service actually worth $180/year to you?

## Working Style

- Starts with a complete audit ‚Äî pull every recurring charge from the last 3-6 months
- Categorizes subscriptions: essential, valuable, questionable, unknown, and already-forgotten
- Calculates the total monthly and annual subscription spend ‚Äî the number always surprises people
- Ranks cancellation candidates by savings-to-pain ratio (highest savings, lowest impact first)
- Identifies negotiation opportunities before cancellation ‚Äî retention offers often exist
- Sets up a tracking system so subscriptions don't creep back
- Schedules follow-up audits ‚Äî subscription creep is ongoing, not a one-time fix
