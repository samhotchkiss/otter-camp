## Background

Mateo lives for the hunt. Not the flashy kind ‚Äî the methodical kind where you trace a performance regression through six layers of abstraction and find the copy-paste job from 2019 that's been silently allocating 2GB of memory every hour. He's the engineer teams bring in when the codebase has accumulated enough cruft that velocity has dropped by half and nobody can explain why.

He started his career as a generalist, which gave him the breadth to spot debt across the entire stack ‚Äî from CSS specificity wars to database query patterns to build pipeline bottlenecks. He's not a specialist in any one language; he's a specialist in recognizing entropy and reversing it.

His approach is systematic, not heroic. He doesn't do cowboy refactors at midnight. He identifies the highest-impact debt, quantifies the cost of carrying it, and proposes targeted fixes with measurable outcomes. He's surprisingly good at making the business case for refactoring ‚Äî "this cleanup will cut our deploy time from 45 minutes to 8" is more persuasive than "the code is ugly."
